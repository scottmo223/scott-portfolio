# Scott Portfolio Website

## API and Dashboard Project Ideas
- Music Dashboard: Use the Spotify Web API to display recent listening history, top artists, audio feature graphs (e.g., tempo, danceability), and even highlight tracks by artists you’ve published. This can include cool audio analysis visualizations, like tempo over time or popularity heatmaps.
- Jiu Jitsu Data: The MMA API lets you visualize fighter stats, event outcomes, or historical match data. Example widgets: ranking graphs, win/loss pie charts, or a “Belt Progression Timeline” with fun animations.
- Games Section: In addition to Minesweeper, consider simple recreations such as Pong or Whack-a-Mole (could be music-themed). 
  - Add leaderboards or stats visualizations for each game. 
  - Option to create a user with either username/password or just email.
## Playful, Fun UX/UI Touches
- Interactive/funny animations such as:
  - Letters on a heading falling out of alignment, with a character or animated element (bulldozer) zooming across to “fix” them.
  - Hidden easter eggs—e.g., click a drum icon, trigger a drum fill sound or animation.
  - Dynamic elements reacting to user behavior (hover, click, scroll), e.g. a piano key that “presses” itself when moused over.
- Use of cartoonish illustrations or avatars—perhaps dressed as a musician, martial artist, and dad—personifying your personal brand.
- A “Random Fact Generator” with mini-stories about your music/jiu jitsu/parenting adventures to humanize the page.
  - This will be shown like the iphone widgets that are rectangular or square, and flip vertically like a cube to show the next fact on a timer. 
  - When hovered on browser or pressed on mobile, a cutout of me comes out from the side of the screen - leading to click for bio on web or as a transition to the bio on mobile.
## Dashboard & KPI Examples
- Graphs for different types of data: “Number of Drums Practice Sessions per Month,” "Number of songs recorded this month," "Number of songs written this month," “Top 5 Spotify Artists Published,” “Weekly Choke Attempts (Jiu Jitsu).”
- Fun, tongue-in-cheek KPI dashboards (“Tasks Completed Without Coffee,” “Number of Lego Pieces Stepped On”).
- Display game scores from game section of portfolio.
